Hiya,

I'd better explain. Poya is the Buddhist full moon holiday in Sri Lanka, a bit like Sunday used to be in the UK - you can't do anything (particularly if it involves unguents, see Precept 7 below). It seemed a good time to let you know of developments in our Sri Lanka trip. Yes, Caroline and I are definitely going on the 4th Sept, all being well staying for a year. Flight booked, bags packed (not), house let (not), accomodation arranged (pretty much). When we arrive we'll be staying with Mr. Silva at the Randomba Inn, Randombe near Ambalangoda.

Mr. Silva at Ambalangoda market

Apart from Colombo (noisy traffic city) and the North and East regions (there's a war on), the island is like Paradise. It's very near the equator and the temperature is mid-70s/mid-80s all year round.

Yours truly at the Randomba Inn

 

There are two monsoon seasons, one in the NE and one in the SW, and we should be arriving just at the tail end of the southern rains.

A cinnamon essential oil still, on 'Big Island' near Ambalangoda

 

War etc.

Although there's a full blown civil war going on in the North & East, we didn't see much evidence of this in the South apart from the occasional roadblock. It's very hard to tell what's actually going on - the press can't say a lot. There's quite a good source of news at 'Lanka Page' - they have stories from different papers in the region (often completely contradictory).

One less obvious danger area is cricket matches :

COLOMBO: A local umpire officiating in a village cricket tournament at the southern coastal town of Ambalangoda last Sunday was clubbed to death by players over a disputed run out, the Daily Mirror reported on Thursday. The newspaper, quoting police officials, said the umpire, identified as W Kamal Ranjith De Silva, was rushed to hospital after he was beaten hard with iron bars by three players following a heated argument over a runout decision. De Silva was a member of the southern provincial council Softball Cricket Umpires Association. The last match he officiated was organised by a well-known club in the area. Two people were arrested in this connection and hunt was on for others, police said. (PTI)

Stuff

You're allowed 20kg main luggage + 7kg hand luggage on the flight. I've got myself a new laptop (it's brill!), and Caro's got a slim scanner & printer (and my old laptop) for her class preparation. So that's the hand luggage. I packed a bag of tools, including a new tenon saw and a brace & bit I bought specially - straight over 20kg. Then there's the books. Books are not light. Computer books are bloody heavy. We've each sent a parcel ahead - I sent 14kg of books, by sea - about £60. So I'm down to my favourite gouges and a couple of obscure AI books I'd have trouble getting hold of out there. Clothes - well I've got a string vest...

Spent a couple of hours a day for the last week trying to consolidate the various bits of software that I'll be likely to want, get them onto a dozen or so CDs. It's mainly downloads, written on CDs with labels like 'Friday' and 'Bits'. Still got a long way to go.

Not much else to say really, so here's a bit more about Poya :

A Buddhist temple

 

A practising Buddhist observes the poya day by visiting a temple for the rituals of worship and, often, by undertaking the Eight Precepts. The Eight Precepts include the Five Precepts (the first 5 below), with the third changed to abstinence from unchastity, and the following three additional rules:

(1) I undertake the precept to abstain from destroying life

Ok.

(2) I undertake the precept to abstain from taking things not given.

Fine.

(3) I undertake the precept to abstain from sexual misconduct.

hur hur

(4) I undertake the precept to abstain from false speech.

No problem. True!

(5) I undertake the precept to abstain from taking distilled and fermented liquors that cause intoxication and heedlessness.

Which in Sinhalese is : Suramerayamajjapamadatthana veramani sikkhapadam samadiyami.

Must remember that.

(6) to abstain from solid food after mid-day;

Tricky, given (5)

(7) to abstain from dancing, singing, music, and improper shows, and from ornamenting the body with garlands, scents, unguents, etc.;

Shouldn't be too difficult, except perhaps for the unguents...

(8) to abstain from the use of high and luxurious beds and seats.

Come on - who made that one up - MFI?

 

Source : Buddhist Ceremonies and Rituals of Sri Lanka

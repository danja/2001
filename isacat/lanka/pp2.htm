Vap Poya - Ambalangoda, Sri Lanka

I'd pretty much given up on this web host, so I sent some piccies as attachments for this poya. Finally seems to be working again, so here are the piccies, with the notes I put in the mail ::

the layout of where we're staying, we're currently using 1 as a bedroom/office(!) and 2 (which needs some work) as a kitchen/classroom(!!), but soon we're just going to have room 2 - the purple dashes are a rough fence and the route the path to the beach will take (once we've finished the garden)

 

 

me outside the bedroom

 

Caroline standing on the rock marked on the map. The beach disappeared for a while, monsoon weather took the sea right up to the edge of the garden, but now it's returned, revealing a load more good rocks to sit on. window.jpg - view out of the bedroom window. My desk (where I'm sat now) is in the corner just to the right off the picture.

 

 

me gardening. Got rid of most of that stuff ('rana'?), except lots of *very* stubborn flying roots.

 

I was working away happily on the laptop until I saw this fellow inches from my arm. Most of its body is over the desk corner - it was actually about 4 1/2 ft long. The photo is blurred because I was shaking somewhat. Not venomous, allegedly.

 

 

All's well here, still not out of monsoon season fully yet - overcast & a bit of rain today. Most days are (very) hot & sunny though (most of this batch of photo's were 1/2 bleached out and 1/2 virtually black, despite using decent film - the local developer is called "Edwin's", by the way - good Derbyshire name). Getting on with work ok, (me programming/writing, Caroline teaching English) despite unusual distractions - apart from the visitor in the picture, we also had a bat in the kitchen, flap, flap, flap, and have had sizable monitor lizards in the garden, as well regular squirrels, occasional huge fruitbats and a mongoose in the garden, and there was a scorpion in one of the other rooms. There's something living in the roof space, Mr. Silva thinks it's a wild cat, but I rather think it's squirrels or perhaps a bat. Then there's the (welcome) geckos on the bedroom walls - caught one crunching a huge beetle last night, and of course mosquitos, flies and ants in abundance. I've spent the last month making a workbench, should have it finished in a couple of weeks then I can get on with some carving. Caro's been learning web design in her spare time.

Cheery bio,

Danny.

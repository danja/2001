JavaHelp

Even if you have not used JavaHelp itself, the end product will not be unfamiliar as the system provides a set of tools found in many other applications, such as Windows Help. We are not be concerned here with the detailed workings of JavaHelp, but with its mode of storing data.

We are going to use JavaHelp to provide a system looking something like this :

snake1.bmp

Rather than a help system in the usual sense, called up from within a parent application, here we are using JavaHelp as a standalone application to present an electronic book from the 'Programmer to Zookeeper' line. In this Contents view the panel on the left shows the available documents in a navigable tree layout. Double-clicking on the folders causes them to reveal or hide their contents, clicking on the lollipop icon cause the tree to expand or contract. The panel on the right displays the document currently selected in the left-hand panel. At the top of the panel on the left are another two more tabs, clicking the next of these shows the Index view :

snake2.bmp

In this view the list on the left can be scrolled down the list to the entry we are interested in, or the term we are looking for may be typed into the Find: text entry box. When the required term is highlighted on the left, the corresponding document is displayed on the right. It is usual to create the index in alphabetical order, though this is entirely optional, as is the order of the folders in the Contents view. If any of the material in the panels extends beyond the visible area, a scrollbar is provided.

The third tab is for a search view, the indexing tools for which are already integrated into JavaHelp, so aren't of much interest here. Later however we will briefly show how this facility is used.

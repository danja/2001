Old Stuff

Cool URIs don't change, and uncool ones do.

The Internet Archive has some of my stuff dating back to March 1997 with the site name "Telephone Demon" - this version is from 2001 when it seems like I had most material online: 10 pages. The site name came about because hosting came free with demon.co.uk and you had to do it over the phone...try to think of an interesting word when you're on the phone.

I did have another site set up around the same period I called "Intermittent", but as far as I remember there wasn't very much by way of content - but it did have a very stylish (er, dated, frames-based) front page. That one's dropped off the Web altogether (looks like its URIs were http://www.danja.free-online.co.uk/ - not archived, and http://members.xoom.com/dayers - robots.txt blocked).

Fast-forward to around 2000 and I had (at least) two domain names registered, but now those domains are long gone. What's rather irritating is that the new owners of the domains (name-grabbers) have robots.txt in place so the Internet Archive doesn't have a copy.

But I did keep a backup on CD (labelled 29/09/2001), so here they are (under this domain): isacat.net and citnames.com. (I've tweaked the front pages a minute amount, the rest is as-was).

Literal

rdfs:Literal

The most primitive value type represented in RDF, typically a string of characters. The content of a literal is not interpreted by RDF itself and may contain additional XML markup. Literals are distinguished from Resources in that the RDF model does not permit literals to be the subject of a statement.

Resource

rdfs:Resource

An abstract object that represents either a physical object such as a person or a book or a conceptual object such as a color or the class of things that have colors. Web pages are usually considered to be physical objects, but the distinction between physical and conceptual or abstract objects is not important to RDF. A resource can also be a component of a larger object; for example, a resource can represent a specific person's left hand or a specific paragraph out of a document. As used in this specification, the term resource refers to the whole of an object if the URI does not contain a fragment (anchor) id or to the specific subunit named by the fragment or anchor id.

About this Glossary

Accuracy
This nascent technology draws from many different traditions each with their own specialised vocabulary. In many cases a term may be common to more than one vocabulary, and depending on the context the intended meaning may vary considerably. The definitions listed here are only intended as guidance to the most common application of the terms in this field. In other words absolutely no guarantee is given for the accuracy of the definitions listed here.

Source Material
In many cases a working definition has simply been copy & pasted from another document, which will be cleaned and polished as time permits. If you see some of your own words here, please don't take offence, mail me and I'll add an acknowledgement or remove the material if you prefer.

Scope
The scope of this document is loosely defined as being information relevant to the Semantic Web - if a term isn't specific to this domain then it won't be included unless it is considered particularly important in this context. If a term in common use is ambiguous or poorly defined but has a precise meaning within the context of the Semantic Web, it will also be considered suitable for inclusion here. This document is not intended as a schema repository - terms from RDF & RDFS are included for obvious reasons, and also elements of Dublin Core as this can be considered archetypal metadata (and it gets used a lot ;-).

Links
In the definition of a term, a word or phrase may be hyperlinked elsewhere. If there is a corresponding definition within this document, then that will be linked to in the first instance and off-site definitions will be made available within the particular term's entry.

Capitalisation & Spelling
If the term appears in a namespace with a lower-case first letter, this will be reflected in the listing here. Spelling will follow those generally found on the web, in particular at the W3C
